# On the Comfort of Immutability

The reduction of the most abhorrent acts to a supposedly innate nature of man underlies only the most cowardly of philosophies. In their flight from accountability, those who fancy themselves real connoisseurs of true human nature find every chance at change foreclosed. All things could be traced back to the inherent wickedness of man, without a single one having to take the word "responsibility" into their mouth.

That same man, whose maltreatment of morality has already dug him his grave, may look his neighbour in the face and attempt to explain to him that his misery is unstoppable, inevitable even— as though there were nothing on earth that could be changed. His conscience remains unmoved; he believes he has no other choice. His inability to help those around him leaves his worldview barren and dry, and his inability to confess this leaves him morally bankrupt.
