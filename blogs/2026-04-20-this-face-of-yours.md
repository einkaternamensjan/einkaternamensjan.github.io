# The Face of Yours

**"Do you also still have a clock in your head?"** A clock that measures what cannot be measured. 
The waltz, stoic, as always. _"Time passes, the day dissolves."_
Where did this figure come from? Ground-down skin lies upon the spirit. My face, yours. Your face, mine. 
Far away, looked into the eye. The children of what we no longer dare to behold.
I cut it off, tear it out. Inverse glimmer, dark little spots, holes in the soul. 
Let us settle it — meet me here. Behind the curtain — no, the pretense of cowardice.
Your face, mine, I no longer recognize — yet, what lurks behind it?
My face, yours, grinds itself down. What could be known, where could be known.
**Heavenly, as everything here, without flaw, without objection**. I leave again, far away, let you suffer, learn too.
The faces, ours, decay, decompose — have they always been this way? The true self, the self of the one, flutters hesitantly through that night.
Since time immemorial — dark men, the sugar of the summit, liquidated.
The bulb of the head, hidden in the brain, the fourfold of those, with their countenances at the core.
I return, repulsive to you, revolting and pale. The devil's point of view, Beelzebub. Tender, timid, the young sense of self.
To throw the die, to hope for good fortune. We are inside the die, dominion as if. 

The sweetness of sugar resurfaces — *innocence is void, when the world is made of guilt.*
