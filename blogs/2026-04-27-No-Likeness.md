# No Likeness

Grass, a meadow. Trees in the distance, you look around. Sunny, warm. The morning dew still lingers. On this meadow you are alone, master of the situation. Flowers bloom, tulips, crocuses. Bees hum in the sunshine. Today you look so beautiful. The hair braided. The eyes glimmer in the light of day. Your jacket so magnificent, chosen by the beloved. The skirt flows, like the water in the river. On the other side of the meadow you notice a building. A barn of a farmstead, or perhaps the farm itself. You move, the air lukewarm. To approach the building is what you want. So you press forward. Grasshoppers leap, whirring about. You hear again the humming of the bees nearby. As you draw closer to the building, you feel tense, what could be inside after all this time. An eternity passes, you gradually arrive. Lights, from the gaps between the planks. From these there comes a softer sound. What happened here? tens of thousands of years ago. Have you arrived? Good. Four walls, and a roof, of the strongest wood. Is that oak? you ask yourself. But is that not beautiful! Something hisses, within those walls. You wave it off, circle the building. The desire to reach inside. The urge to remain outside. Have you arrived? Good. You tenderly stroke the wood of one of the four walls, old, but still in an excellent condition. Who was once at work here? you wonder. A door, how wonderful, now you can finally go in, to find out what once came to fruition here. The warm sun, an escape without end. The sound grows steadily louder. The light grows brighter. Is there even electricity here? The sound is of chattering and laughter. The laughter of a child. The parents exchange words. Deceiving themselves in the process. It has no smell, otherwise you would smell it. It has no taste, otherwise you would taste it. It makes no sounds, otherwise you would perceive it. It has no senses, otherwise you would understand it. All at once you begin to barricade the windows. The door must close, but how are you going to manage that??? You were always susceptible to fits of rage.

NOW ENOUGH OF THE THINGS YOU CONSIDER BEAUTIFUL.

It is so large, so infinitely large. It is so wide, so infinitely wide. It is so tall, so infinitely tall. It is so dense, so infinitely dense. It is time, finally time. It knows no boundaries, no end in sight. It cannot have them, otherwise you would grasp it. Everything is too narrow for it, so unfathomably unbearably narrow. Everything is too large for it, so unfathomably unbearably large. You look sweaty, or merely heavily wept? It weeps too, don't you worry about that. It weeps alone, weeps in the night, weeps by day, weeps so softly. You are not alone in that building. Former inmates surround you. Are they as vivid as you once imagined? Six million hearts, in that chest, countless millions of teeth; in the frenzy of the mortuary, you err around. You feel the tension again, pressure from within, from without nothing at all. No eye, no light, no heart, no nothing. You are welcome to do everything to prevent it. It WILL happen. Unexpected and suddenly. Make yourself safe. Hide yourself well too. You still have time, before the embers. Expected, as ordered, it is here. No worries, no fear, remaining in you. You scream, yes, shriek your throat raw, tear it out, whereupon you crush it in your hand.

NOW ENOUGH OF EVERYTHING YOU CONSIDER VALUABLE.

The eyes with which you behold the world are my prey, my unfathomable, infinite reward. Through me you will see everything, be able to truly behold everything. Are we not one and the same? The recovery and the illness? To the pounding heart the blood refuses itself. Beyond heaven, this side of hell I am not to be conjured. The night of the moths passes, the storm rises, you grow cold, so unbearably cold. Let reality present no obstacle to us.

NOW ENOUGH OF THIS DAMNED FINITUDE.

Now finally **the finite** is infinite. The reach of its power, it devours the world, see how easily it executes this! It has no eyes, no ears, no qualities. It is not tangible, not objectlike.
