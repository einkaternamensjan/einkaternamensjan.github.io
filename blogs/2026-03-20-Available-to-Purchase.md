The mass production of commodities brings about the mass production of the self. The human being, who has themselves become a commodity, can only identify with the products they purchase. The one who still insists on using a map calls themselves a "map person" in order to be able to bear the non-being of their self. In this world of supposedly free choice, even the contours of identity have become blurred. Where does the person end and where does the commodity begin? In a society without this infinite "agony of choice," the human being would have to confront their greatest source of anxiety, namely themselves; from this confrontation with oneself emerges the horror of death. Bare existence itself is the commodity-person's death.
